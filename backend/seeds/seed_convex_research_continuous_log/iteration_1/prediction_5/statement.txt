(Geometric decay of per-iteration decrease ratios.) Let f be \mu-strongly convex and L-smooth, and let \eta\in(0,\tfrac{2}{L}). Define r_n:=\tfrac{f_{n+1}-f_\star}{f_n-f_\star} and q:=\max\{|1-\eta\lambda|^2:\lambda\in[\mu,L]\}. Then r_n is nonincreasing for all n and \lim_{n\to\infty} r_n=q. In particular, the instantaneous linear rate tightens monotonically to the spectral radius bound.
