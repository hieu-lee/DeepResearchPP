[
    "Let $\{y_t\}$ be a target series and suppose we have $m$ unbiased base forecasters with
    errors $e_t = (\hat y_{1,t}-y_t,\ldots,\hat y_{m,t}-y_t)^\top$.
    Assume time is partitioned into $R$ regimes (due to structural breaks), and in each regime $r$
    the error vector is mean-zero with covariance $\Sigma_r \in \mathbb{R}^{m\times m}$.

    Among all linear unbiased combinations $\tilde y_{t}=w^\top\hat y_t$ with $\mathbf{1}^\top w=1$,
    the MSE-minimizing weights in regime $r$ are
    $$
    w_r^\star \;=\; \frac{\Sigma_r^{-1}\mathbf{1}}{\mathbf{1}^\top \Sigma_r^{-1}\mathbf{1}}.
    $$",
    "
    Let $\hat\Sigma_r$ denote a regime-specific estimator of $\Sigma_r$ obtained by
    (i) removing common factors from $e_t$ (factor-adjustment) and
    (ii) estimating the idiosyncratic precision $\Theta_r=\Sigma_r^{-1}$ with a sparse
    graphical estimator (e.g., Graphical LASSO) on the factor-adjusted residuals.
    Then the **stable** combination weights are
    $$
    \hat w_r \;=\; \frac{\hat\Theta_r\,\mathbf{1}}{\mathbf{1}^\top \hat\Theta_r\,\mathbf{1}},
    \qquad\text{with}\quad \hat\Theta_r \;=\; \arg\max_{\Theta\succeq 0}
    \;\Big\{\log\det\Theta - \mathrm{tr}(\hat S_r\Theta) - \lambda\|\Theta\|_{1}\Big\},
    $$
    where $\hat S_r$ is the sample covariance of factor-adjusted errors in regime $r$,
    and $\lambda>0$ controls sparsity.  A ridge-stabilized variant is
    $$
    \hat w_{r,\lambda} \;=\; \frac{(\hat\Sigma_r + \lambda I)^{-1}\mathbf{1}}
    {\mathbf{1}^\top (\hat\Sigma_r + \lambda I)^{-1}\mathbf{1}}.
    $$
    "
